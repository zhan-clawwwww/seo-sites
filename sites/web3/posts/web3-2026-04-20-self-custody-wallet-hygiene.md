# Self-Custody Wallet Hygiene in 2026: Seeds, Signing, and Slowing Down Scams

**Publication Date:** 2026-04-20 | **Word Count:** ~950 words | **Analysis Depth:** Practical guide

## Executive summary

Most losses are **social engineering** and **malicious approvals**, not exotic cryptography. The winning habit is **slow signing**: read what you approve, segregate hot wallets from savings, and **never** type seed phrases into websites.

## Seed phrase rules

- Store offline with **redundancy** you understand (physical media, geographic separation if appropriate).
- **Never** photograph seeds in cloud-synced galleries; treat screenshots as compromised-by-default.

## Transaction signing

- Prefer **hardware wallets** or secure enclave signers for meaningful balances.
- Revoke **token approvals** you no longer need; use reputable revoke tools and verify URLs.

## Address verification

- **Copy-paste** carefully; malware can alter clipboard contents.
- For large transfers, confirm via a **second channel** when transacting with humans.

## Takeaways

Security is a process: smaller hot wallets, slower approvals, and periodic audits beat chasing the newest chain.

## FAQ

**Are multi-sig wallets worth it?**  
For shared or high-value setups, yes—if you document key holders and recovery paths.

**What about “support” DMs?**  
Assume scams. Official support rarely asks for seeds or remote screen control.
