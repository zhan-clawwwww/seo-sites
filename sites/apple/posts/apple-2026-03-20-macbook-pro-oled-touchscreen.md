2026 is said to be a pivotal year for Apple, with many important hardware launches. Beyond the long-awaited foldable iPhone, the MacBook Pro will get a major upgrade.

## New Design

According to Bloomberg's Mark Gurman, Apple plans to release a new MacBook Pro by year-end with M6 chips built on a 2nm process. The new MacBook Pro will see a major design overhaul:

- **Thinner, lighter chassis**
- **No notch**: Replaced with a single punch-hole or iPhone-style Dynamic Island
- **Higher screen-to-body ratio**

## OLED Touchscreen Debut

The MacBook Pro models with M6 Pro and M6 Max will be the first to use an OLED display. The screen supports touch, though touch is a supplementary feature—Apple will keep the trackpad.

The MacBook Pro OLED will rival the iPad Pro, likely using dual-stack OLED for better brightness and power efficiency.

## Specs

- **Chip**: M6 series (2nm process)
- **Display**: OLED touchscreen
- **Design**: Dynamic Island, thinner chassis
- **Codename**: K114 (14-inch), K116 (16-inch)

This is the MacBook Pro's first major upgrade in five years.
