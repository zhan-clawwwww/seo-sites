# Apple's Busy March: iPhone 17e Launches, iOS 26.4 Arrives with Security Fixes

Apple has had one of its busiest Marches in years, launching multiple products and issuing critical security updates. Here's everything that happened and what it means for users.

## iPhone 17e: Affordable Power

On March 2, 2026, Apple unveiled the iPhone 17e, a powerful and affordable addition to the iPhone 17 lineup. The "e" suffix follows Apple's tradition of offering budget-friendly options without sacrificing core capabilities.

The iPhone 17e sits alongside the standard iPhone 17 and iPhone 17 Pro models, giving consumers more choice in Apple's smartphone range. While it may lack some premium features, it brings modern performance to a lower price point.

## iOS 26.4: Update Now

Apple released iOS 26.4 along with a warning to all iPhone users: update immediately. The update contains critical security patches that address vulnerabilities that could be actively exploited.

Forbes reported that the iOS 26.4 upgrade "comes with a warning to update your iPhone now, because it contains a [security fix]." The update also includes eight new emoji and various new iPhone features.

## The Broader March Lineup

Apple's March event was described as "the biggest week of 2026" by MacRumors. Beyond the iPhone 17e, Apple launched:

- New MacBook models with M5 chips
- Updated iPads
- OLED display improvements
- Various iOS updates

9to5Mac noted that Apple's busy March continued with "three notable arrivals this week" even after the initial product blitz.

## Family Sharing Improvements

When iOS 26.4 releases, Apple will finally let adult Family Sharing members use their own payment methods for purchases. Currently, all purchases route through the family organizer's payment method.

## Security Vulnerabilities

The urgency around iOS 26.4 updates highlights ongoing security challenges. Earlier in March, a newer version of the DarkSword exploit targeting iOS 18.4 to 18.6.2 appeared on GitHub.

With about 34 percent of iPhone and 43 percent of iPad users still running older iOS versions, Apple faces an ongoing challenge in getting users to update.
