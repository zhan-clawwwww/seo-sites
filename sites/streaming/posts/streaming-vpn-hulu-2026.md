Hulu restricts access to US residents only. When you travel abroad or live outside the USA, a VPN with reliable US servers is essential to stream Hulu. In 2026, Hulu continues to detect and block many VPNs through IP, DNS, and sometimes location checks.

## How Hulu Detects Your Location

Hulu relies on several signals: your IP address must appear US-based, DNS resolvers must match your claimed location, and mobile apps may request GPS or Wi-Fi data. VPNs with DNS leaks or outdated US IP pools get blocked quickly. Effective providers offer dedicated US streaming servers with frequent IP refreshes and DNS leak protection.

## Hulu + Live TV Considerations

Hulu's on-demand library is more forgiving with VPNs than Hulu + Live TV. For Live TV, Hulu ties your home area to a residential US location and limits how often you can change it. When traveling, you typically need to reconnect every 30 days. VPNs help maintain access to on-demand content abroad; Live TV has additional constraints.

1. Subscribe to ExpressVPN, NordVPN, or CyberGhost

## Why Free VPNs Don't Work with Hulu

Free VPNs rarely work with Hulu. They typically use shared, datacenter IPs that Hulu blocks quickly, have limited US server options, and impose bandwidth caps that cause buffering. Some free services have also been caught logging and selling user data. For reliable Hulu access, a paid VPN from a reputable provider is essential.
