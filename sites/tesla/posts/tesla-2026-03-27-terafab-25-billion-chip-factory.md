# Tesla and SpaceX Launch $25 Billion TERAFAB Chip Factory

Elon Musk officially launched TERAFAB on March 21, 2026 — a $25 billion joint chip fabrication venture between Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI. The ambitious project aims to produce one terawatt of AI compute annually from its Austin, Texas facility.

## What is TERAFAB?

TERAFAB represents Tesla's biggest bet yet on vertical integration for AI hardware. The facility will manufacture both terrestrial inference chips for Tesla's autonomous driving systems and space-hardened processors for SpaceX's satellite constellation.

The project was first confirmed during Tesla's January 28, 2026 earnings call, with Musk describing it as essential for the company's long-term AI ambitions.

## The Scale of the Project

The numbers are staggering:

- **Investment:** $25 billion over multiple years
- **Target Output:** 1 terawatt of AI compute annually
- **Location:** Austin, Texas
- **Partners:** Tesla, SpaceX, xAI

For context, this single facility would aim to produce more AI computing capacity than many entire countries currently possess.

## Why It Matters

Tesla's autonomous driving systems require enormous computational resources, both for training AI models and for running inference in their vehicles. Currently, Tesla relies on external chip suppliers like NVIDIA.

By bringing chip manufacturing in-house, Tesla aims to:

1. **Reduce costs** through vertical integration
2. **Customize chips** specifically for Tesla's AI workloads
3. **Secure supply** amid global chip shortages
4. **Compete directly** with NVIDIA and other chip makers

## Skepticism and Challenges

Analysts have noted that the project faces significant challenges:

- **Cost:** $25 billion is a massive investment
- **Timeline:** Building chip fabs typically takes years
- **Expertise:** Tesla has limited semiconductor manufacturing experience
- **Competition:** Established players like TSMC and Samsung have decades of experience

Electrek described the project as "Battery Day on steroids — and even less realistic," reflecting the skepticism some industry observers feel.

## Regulatory Headwinds

Meanwhile, Tesla faces increased regulatory scrutiny. The company is now under a wider probe of its self-driving features as it prepares to sell vehicles without steering wheels, raising new safety questions.

## Stock Market Reaction

Despite the skepticism, Tesla's stock rose on March 23 as investors rallied around the company's potential long-term AI roadmap. The market appears willing to give Musk the benefit of the doubt, at least for now.
