The reason is simple: running a global server network costs real money. If a provider charges $2/month but operates 5,000 servers across 90 countries, something has to give — and that "something" is usually your privacy.

**The sweet spot in 2026**: Quality VPNs in the $2–5/month range (on annual plans) deliver AES-256 encryption, verified no-logs policies, and speeds fast enough for 4K streaming. You don't need to pay premium prices for premium security.

## Features You Should Never Sacrifice

Even when hunting for the cheapest VPN, these features are non-negotiable:

**1. AES-256 Encryption (or Equivalent)**
This is the industry standard used by governments and banks. If a VPN offers weaker encryption to cut costs, walk away. In 2026, AES-256 is available even in $2/month plans.

**2. Kill Switch**
A kill switch blocks all internet traffic if your VPN connection drops. Without it, your real IP address could be exposed for seconds — enough time for ISPs or hackers to log your activity. Every VPN on our list includes this feature.

**3. No-Logs Policy (Independently Audited)**
Many VPNs claim "no logs" but haven't proven it. Look for independent audits from firms like Deloitte, PricewaterhouseCoopers, or Cure53. All five providers above have undergone such audits.

**4. DNS Leak Protection**
Without this feature, your DNS queries (the websites you visit) could bypass the VPN tunnel, exposing your browsing history. Our testing confirmed all five providers leak no DNS information.

**5. Modern Protocol Support (WireGuard)**
WireGuard offers faster speeds and better security than older protocols like PPTP or L2TP/IPsec. In 2026, any VPN worth using should support WireGuard or its variants.

## Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

### Renewal Pricing Traps
We covered this earlier, but it bears repeating: nearly every VPN offers steep discounts for the first billing cycle, then charges 3–6x more on renewal. Budget for the renewal price, not the introductory one.

### Data Caps on "Unlimited" Plans
Some providers advertise "unlimited" data but throttle speeds after a certain threshold (typically 10–50 GB). This is more common with free tiers but occasionally appears in budget plans. Read the fine print.

### Currency Conversion and Regional Pricing
Some VPNs offer different prices based on your detected location. Users in developing countries might see lower prices, while those in the US or EU pay more. Consider using a VPN to compare prices (ethically).

### Upselling and Add-ons
Basic VPN subscriptions sometimes exclude features like dedicated IP addresses, port forwarding, or advanced malware protection — available only as paid add-ons. Check what's included in your base plan.
