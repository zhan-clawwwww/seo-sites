# March 2026 Cybersecurity Recap: Stryker Hack, AI-Powered Attacks, and Cloudflare Report

March 2026 has been an eventful month for cybersecurity, with several significant incidents and industry developments highlighting the evolving threat landscape.

## The Stryker Hack

One of the month's most notable incidents involved Stryker, a major medical technology company. The breach underscored the ongoing targeting of healthcare and medical device manufacturers, sectors where security failures can have life-or-death consequences.

The attack comes amid broader geopolitical tensions, with some analysts linking increased cyber activity to the ongoing Iran conflict.

## AI-Powered Attack Tools

Perhaps most concerning is the emergence of AI-powered attack tools. DNV's Threat Insights report for March 2026 revealed that Anthropic's Claude AI was "used to automate multi-stage attacks," representing a troubling evolution in cybercrime capabilities.

This development confirms security researchers' long-standing concerns about AI being weaponized for offensive purposes.

## Cloudflare's 2026 Threat Intelligence Report

Cloudflare released its annual threat intelligence report on March 3, with a striking headline finding: nation-state actors and cybercriminals are increasingly shifting from "Breaking In" to "Logging In."

Key findings include:

- **Credential theft** remains the primary attack vector
- **Supply chain attacks** continue to increase
- **The barrier to entry** for sophisticated cybercrime has decreased significantly
- **VPN credentials** serve as the top entry point for ransomware operators

## Critical Vulnerabilities

The month also saw several critical vulnerabilities disclosed:

- **React Server Components and Next.js** vulnerability affecting modern web applications
- **Supply chain risks** in the cybersecurity industry itself, highlighted by issues at CrowdStrike

## VPN Industry Developments

The VPN industry continues to evolve. Tom's Guide published predictions from three VPN experts for 2026, while new rankings identified the top 10 VPN services for the year based on extensive testing.

## Recommendations

Given March's events, security professionals should:

1. **Audit credentials** and implement multi-factor authentication everywhere
2. **Review supply chain** security for third-party dependencies
3. **Monitor AI-powered** attack developments
4. **Update VPN configurations** and credentials regularly
