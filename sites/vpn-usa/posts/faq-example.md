## Getting Started

### What does a VPN actually do?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) encrypts your internet connection and routes it through a server in a location of your choice. This does two things: (1) hides your real IP address from websites and online services, and (2) encrypts your traffic so your ISP, hackers on public WiFi, and other observers can't see what you're doing online.

### Do I really need a VPN in the USA?

You benefit from a VPN if you use public WiFi, want to stream content while traveling, value online privacy, or want to prevent your ISP from selling your browsing data. US ISPs are legally allowed to sell your browsing history to advertisers — a VPN prevents this.

### Which VPN is best for beginners?

NordVPN and Surfshark have the most user-friendly interfaces. Both offer one-click connection, automatic server selection, and helpful setup guides. Both also offer 30-day money-back guarantees, so you can try without risk.

## Privacy & Security

### What is a no-logs VPN policy?

A no-logs policy means the VPN provider doesn't record your browsing activity, connection timestamps, IP addresses, or other identifying information. The best providers have their no-logs claims verified by independent third-party audits from firms like PwC, Deloitte, or Cure53.

### Can the government see my VPN traffic?

With a properly configured no-logs VPN, even if authorities compel the provider to hand over data, there's nothing to provide. This is why jurisdiction matters — NordVPN (Panama) and ExpressVPN (British Virgin Islands) are outside US legal jurisdiction.

### Does a VPN protect me on public WiFi?

Yes, this is one of the most important VPN use cases. Public WiFi networks (hotels, airports, coffee shops) are easily monitored by attackers. A VPN encrypts all your traffic, making it unreadable even if someone intercepts it.

### What is a kill switch and do I need it?

A kill switch blocks all internet traffic if the VPN connection drops, preventing your real IP from being accidentally exposed. It's essential for torrenting and important for privacy-conscious users. Enable it in your VPN app settings.

## Speed & Performance

### Which VPN protocol is fastest?

WireGuard is the fastest VPN protocol, offering speeds close to your native connection. NordVPN's NordLynx and ExpressVPN's Lightway are WireGuard-based. Always choose WireGuard when available — it's both faster and more secure than OpenVPN.

## Setup & Technical Questions

### How do I set up a VPN on my iPhone?

Download the VPN app from the App Store, sign in, and tap "Connect." iOS VPN apps work identically to desktop versions. Enable the kill switch via Settings → VPN for maximum protection.

### Can I set up a VPN on my router?

Yes. Router-level VPN protects every device on your network. ASUS routers (with Merlin firmware) and DD-WRT/Tomato-compatible routers support VPN client mode. NordVPN has the most comprehensive router setup guides.

### What is split tunneling and should I use it?

Split tunneling lets you choose which apps use the VPN and which connect directly. Use it to route only privacy-sensitive apps through the VPN while keeping gaming or other bandwidth-heavy apps on your direct connection. Most premium VPNs support split tunneling.

## Troubleshooting

### Why does my VPN keep disconnecting?

Common causes: weak WiFi signal (VPN needs stable connection), server overload (switch servers), ISP blocking VPN traffic (try a different protocol or port), or device battery optimization killing background apps (disable battery optimization for the VPN app on mobile).

### Why does Netflix say I'm using a VPN?

Netflix actively blocks VPN IP addresses. Switch to a different US server — especially one labeled "Streaming" or "Netflix optimized." If multiple servers fail, contact the VPN's live chat support and ask which servers currently work with Netflix.

### My VPN shows a US server but websites still show my real location. Why?

This is a DNS leak. Your DNS queries are going to your ISP instead of through the VPN. Enable DNS leak protection in your VPN settings and run a test at dnsleaktest.com. Also disable IPv6 if your VPN doesn't support it, as IPv6 can leak your real location.
